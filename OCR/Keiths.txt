A
Stands for Acrobat,
So agile and quick;
Who poses and smiles
After each wondrous trick.

B
For the Ballet, “Starlight”— 
Whose beauty and grace
No other stage picture
Can ever efface.

C
Is for Clown,
That jolly old soul,
Who’s never a bore
With his humor so droll.

D
Stands for Dog,
Just plain D-O-G,
Though quite a fine actor
He’s gotten to be.

E
Is for Engine Room —
Clean, wholesome and bright
A marvel of neatness —
A wonderful sight.

F
For Fadettes —
Fair, feminine band;
Who play popular music
And opera grand,

G
Stands for Girl —
The matinee queen
In her most stunning raiment
At Keith’s can be seen.

H
For Houdini,
The great Handcuff King,
Who wriggles to freedom
From any old thing.

I
For Inspection —
When every Keith man
From his head to his feet
Must appear spic and span.

J
Stands for Juggler —
Disguised as a tramp,
He juggles most anything,
Even a lamp.

K
Stands for Keith,
The Vaudeville King —
Let’s wish him long life,
While his praises we sing.

L
Stands for Leader,
Who, baton in hand,
While he watches the stage
Is directing the band.

M
For Monologist,
Rich with story and jest,
Makes you laugh till you cry—
To tired brains he brings rest.

N
Is for Necromancer,
Who baffles at will
With his feats of black art
And his wonderful skill.

O
For the Orchestra,
Whose music so bright
Lends sparkle and zest,
While it captivates quite.

P
Stands for Patrons —
Gay youth and old age
Are equally pleased
With the show on Keith’s stage.

Q
Is for Queen —
The best one of all —
The good Queen of Fairies,
So graceful and tall.

R
For Rehearsal,
Without which the show
Would drag in its action.
Be tedious and slow.

S
Stands for Stage,
Where life’s counterpart
Is played by the actor
With consummate art.

T
For the Tree
Of the glad Yuletide
With its tinsel and lights
And its presents beside.

U
Is for Usher,
So courteous, polite —
He helps make your visit
A constant delight.

V
Stands for Vaudeville,
Varied and bright —
A gay pot-pourri
That satisfies quite.

W
For Wreath,
And the laurel you know,
Belongs without question
To the Keith Theatre show,

X
Is for Xmas —
Best day of the year —
It gladdens the heart,
Brings to all much good cheer.

Y
Stands for Young Folks,
Whose parents, you know,
Never worry about them
When they’re at the Keith show.

Z
Is for Zylophone,
Mellow and sweet —
When played by an artist,
A musical treat.
