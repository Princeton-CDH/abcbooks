



<head>A</head>
  <lg>
﻿  <l>A is an Abolitionist—</l>
  <l>A man who wants to free</l>
  <l>The wretched slave—</l>
  <l>and give to all</l>
  <l>An equal liberty.</l></lg>
  </lg>

<head>B</head>
<lg>
  <l>B is a Brother with a skin</l>
  <l>Of somewhat darker hue,</l>
  <l>But in our Heavenly Father’s sight.</l>
  <l>He is as dear as you.</l>
</lg>

<head>C</head><lg>
  <l>C is the Cotton-field, to which</l>
  <l>This injured brother’s driven,</l>
  <l>When, as the white man’s slave, he toils</l>
  <l>From early morn till even.</l></lg>

<head>D</head><lg>
  <l>D is the Driver, cold and stern,</l>
  <l>Who follows, whip in hand,</l>
  <l>To punish those who dare to rest,</l>
  <l>Or disobey command.</l></lg>

<head>E</head><lg>
  <l>E is the Eagle, soaring high;</l>
  <l>An emblem of the free;</l>
  <l>But while we chain our brother man,</l>
  <l>Our type he cannot be.</l></lg>

<head>F</head><lg>
  <l>F is the heart-sick Fugitive,</l>
  <l>The slave who runs away,</l>
  <l>And travels through the dreary night,</l>
  <l>But hides himself by day.</l></lg>

<head>G</head><lg>
  <l>G is the Gong, whose rolling sound,</l>
  <l>Before the morning light,</l>
  <l>Calls up the little sleeping slave,</l>
  <l>To labor until night.</l></lg>

<head>H</head><lg>
  <l>H is the Hound his master trained,</l>
  <l>And called to scent the track.</l>
  <l>Of the unhappy fugitive,</l>
  <l>And bring him trembling back.</l></lg>

<head>I</head><lg>
  <l>I is the Infant, from the arms</l>
  <l>Of its fond mother torn,</l>
  <l>And, at a public auction, sold</l>
  <l>With horses, cows, and corn.</l></lg>

<head>J</head><lg>
  <l>J is the Jail, upon whose floor</l>
  <l>That wretched mother lay,</l>
  <l>Until her cruel master came,</l>
  <l>And carried her away.</l></lg>

<head>K</head><lg>
  <l>K is the Kidnapper,who stole</l>
  <l>That little child and mother—</l>
  <l>Shrieking, it clung around her, but</l>
  <l>He tore them from each other.</l></lg>

<head>L</head><lg>
  <l>L is the Lash, that brutally</l>
  <l>He swung around its head,</l>
  <l>Threatening that “if it cried again,</l>
  <l>He’d whip it till ’twas dead.”</l></lg>

<head>M</head><lg>
  <l>M is the Merchant of the north,</l>
  <l>Who buys what slaves produce—</l>
  <l>So they are stolen, whipped and worked,</l>
  <l>For his, and for our use.</l></lg>

<head>N</head><lg>
  <l>N is the Negro, rambling free</l>
  <l>In his far distant home,</l>
  <l>Delighting ’neath the palm trees’ shade</l>
  <l>And cocoa-nut to roam.</l></lg>

<head>O</head><lg>
  <l>O is the Orange tree, that bloomed</l>
  <l>Beside his cabin door,</l>
  <l>When white men stole him from his home</l>
  <l>To see it never more.</l></lg>

<head>P</head><lg>
  <l>P is the Parent, sorrowing,</l>
  <l>And weeping all alone—</l>
  <l>The child he loved to lean upon,</l>
  <l>His only son, is gone !</l></lg>

<head>Q</head><lg>
  <l>Q is the Quarter, where the slave</l>
  <l>On coarsest food is fed,</l>
  <l>And where, with toil and sorrow worn,</l>
  <l>He seeks his wretched bed.</l></lg>

<head>R</head><lg>
  <l>R is the “Rice-swamp, dank and lone,"</l>
  <l>Where, weary, day by day,</l>
  <l>He labors till the fever wastes</l>
  <l>His strength and life away.</l></lg>

<head>S</head><lg>
  <l>S is the Sugar, that the slave</l>
  <l>Is toiling hard to make,</l>
  <l>To put into your pie and tea,</l>
  <l>Your candy, and your cake.</l></lg>

<head>T</head><lg>
  <l>T is the rank Tobacco plant,</l>
  <l>Raised by slave labor too:</l>
  <l>A poisonous and nasty thing,</l>
  <l>For gentlemen to chew.</l></lg>

<head>U</head><lg>
  <l>U is for Upper Canada,</l>
  <l>Where the poor slave has found</l>
  <l>Rest after all his wanderings,</l>
  <l>For it is British ground !</l></lg>

<head>V</head><lg>
  <l>V is the Vessel, in whose dark,</l>
  <l>Noisome, and stifling hold,</l>
  <l>Hundreds of Africans are packed,</l>
  <l>Brought o’er the seas, and sold.</l></lg>

<head>W</head><lg>
  <l>W is the Whipping post,</l>
  <l>To which the slave is bound,</l>
  <l>While on his naked back,</l>
  <l>the lash Makes many a bleeding wound.</l></lg>

<head>X</head><lg>
  <l>X is for Xerxes, famed of yore;</l>
  <l>A warrior stern was he</l>
  <l>He fought with swords;</l>
  <l>let truth and love Our only weapons be.</l></lg>

<head>Y</head><lg>
  <l>Y is for Youth—the time for all</l>
  <l>Bravely to war with sin;</l>
  <l>And think not it can ever he</l>
  <l>Too early to begin.</l></lg>

<head>Z</head><lg>
  <l>Z is a Zealous man, sincere,</l>
  <l>Faithful, and just, and true;</l>
  <l>An earnest pleader for the slave—</l>
  <l>Will you not be so too ?</l>
</lg>
